Biography

Welcome! I am a PhD Candidate in Political Science at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology. My research focuses on the Comparative Politics of the Middle East, with a particular interest in bureaucracies, governance, and protest. At MIT, I am affiliated with GOV/LAB and Global Diversity Lab.

Before beginning my PhD, I completed a Fulbright Fellowship in Jordan and graduated with a BA from Hamilton College and an MA from Johns Hopkins SAIS, where I received the Christian A. Herter Award for Academic Excellence. Throughout these experiences, I worked as a journalist, researcher, and analyst of the conflict in Syria. Though my given name is Elizabeth, most people call me “Biff,” a name that translates exceptionally poorly outside of English. You can call me whichever name you’d prefer.
